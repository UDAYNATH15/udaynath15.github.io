Research

The Changing Landscape of Plastic Waste Trade and Its Impact on the Environment and Waste Management

With: Thomas L. Marsh

Abstract / Focus: Uses the staggered-adoption synthetic difference-in-differences estimator with satellite-derived PM2.5 measurements at waste-site locations to estimate how China's 2018 National Sword policy and the resulting cascading trade reallocation affected air pollution — decomposing direct reductions in restricting countries from displacement effects in recipient countries with inadequate waste-management capacity.

Spillover Effects of Trade Policies in the Agri-Food Global Value Chain

With: Jeff Luckstead

Abstract / Focus: Uses a GVC-augmented structural gravity model on bilateral value-added data for 190 countries (2000–2017) to identify how the depth of a conduit country's trade-agreement network — not bilateral agreement depth — drives upstream value-added integration in agri-food supply chains, with distinct mechanisms for tariffs vs. deep agreements and sectoral heterogeneity between agriculture and food processing.

Social Costs of Microplastics in Agricultural Production

Advisor: Thomas L. Marsh

Abstract / Focus: Uses a dynamic optimization model with an embedded damage function to estimate the long-run social costs of microplastic accumulation from agricultural plastic mulch — quantifying strawberry yield losses (≈21.6%), quality degradation (≈19.5%), and human-health risks from microplastic uptake into produce.
